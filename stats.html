Statistics

The United State has the largest number of immigration than any other country. In 2017, 1.127 million immigrants entetred the country legally. The majority of people obtaining legal status were from the continents of Asia or other places in North America.

Most of the immigrants comming to America, according to the Department of Home Land Security in 2017, were from the Caribbean or Mexico.

Estimates

Immigration each year increases and is projected to continue to increase for the next 40 years.

Unauthorized Immigration

Many immigrants do not always enter the country legally. In 2016, there were an estimated 10.7 million immigrants who entered the U.S. illegally. Mexican and Central American immigrants make up about two-thirds of the unauthorized immigrants as 2016.

Citizenship

On average it takes about 6 months to become a citizen after filing the N-400, Application for Naturalization.
